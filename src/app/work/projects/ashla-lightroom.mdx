## What it is

Ashla is a plugin for Adobe Lightroom Classic that does your colour grading for you. Describe the look you want, hand it a reference image, or just ask it to make the shot better, and it reads the photo and applies a proper set of develop settings in one click.

## Why it's handy

Normal editing means pushing dozens of sliders around or buying presets that never quite fit the frame. Ashla works out what you're going for and applies a coherent edit, treating all the sliders as one connected system instead of a pile of separate dials.

## What it does

- Three ways in: describe it ("warm Portra 400 tones"), give it a reference image, or hit the automatic "Lucky" mode
- Handles exposure, contrast, tone curves, HSL, colour grading, and grain
- Can reframe, level a horizon, or crop to a film format if you want it to
- Actually looks at the photo (it reads the pixels from a JPEG preview), so its choices fit the image
- Stays restrained on purpose. It's after the grade you don't notice, not an obvious filter

## How it works

The plugin grabs the photo's metadata and a JPEG preview, sends it off to a vision model on a background thread, and gets back a structured set of develop settings. Those get checked, clamped to safe ranges, and applied as a preset.

## Under the hood

Written in Lua for the Lightroom Classic plugin system, backed by an OpenAI vision model, with your API key kept in the OS keychain.
