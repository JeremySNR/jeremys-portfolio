## What it was

AI Mark was a talking AI version of Mark Sewards, the Labour MP for Leeds South West and Morley, and the first time a sitting UK MP had cloned themselves into a chatbot. We built it at Neural Voice, accent and all, so constituents could get hold of "Mark" any time, including when the office was shut. It was never meant to replace the real job, just to take some of the load off the thousands of queries that come in.

## The idea

An MP's inbox never stops. The thinking was simple: let people talk to an always-on version that can answer the common stuff instantly, and free up the human MP and his team for the things that genuinely need them.

## The reaction (this one really kicked off)

This is the one people loved to hate. Critics called it "lazy", "ecologically irresponsible", and called Mark himself a "twit". The BBC ran experts warning that someone turning up with a serious problem deserves better than an impersonal stand-in, and the bot was honest enough to admit it didn't know local priorities. It also kicked off a proper national debate, with plenty of people arguing it was a sensible glimpse of where constituency work is heading.

Either way, an AI we built ended up on the front of the Washington Post and the BBC, and got the whole country arguing about what an MP is actually for. I'll take that.

The MP himself was happy, at least:

> "I can't thank Jeremy Smith and Neural Voice enough for all their hard work to create the UK's first virtual MP!"
>
> Mark Sewards MP

## Where it led

The interesting part is what happened after the headlines moved on. The project opened an ongoing policy dialogue with Mark that has widened well beyond chatbots: we've been working through strategy for UK AI sovereignty, including how the country mitigates the risk of depending on US-controlled frontier models like Anthropic's Mythos and Fable 5 that could be restricted or withdrawn overnight. Building AI Mark also put me in direct conversation with OpenAI's head of policy on a couple of occasions, which is not a bad room to be in for someone who started out cloning an MP's accent.
